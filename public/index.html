About Us

School of Engineering

Egerton University's School of Engineering is a center of excellence in engineering education, research, and innovation in East Africa.

Shaping the Future of Engineering in Kenya

The School of Engineering at Egerton University comprises four dynamic departments, offering programmes accredited by the Engineers Board of Kenya (EBK). Our graduates are leaders in infrastructure development, renewable energy, agricultural mechanization, and technology innovation across Africa.

Electrical & Electronic Engineering

Power systems, electronics, telecommunications, and control engineering

Civil & Environmental Engineering

Structural, geotechnical, water resources, and environmental engineering

Mechanical & Manufacturing Engineering

Thermodynamics, mechanics, manufacturing, and industrial engineering

Agricultural & Biosystems Engineering

Farm mechanization, food processing, soil & water conservation

Innovation Hub

Student-led maker spaces and innovation labs for prototyping solutions to real-world challenges.

EBK Accredited

All programmes accredited by the Engineers Board of Kenya for professional registration.

Industry Links

Partnerships with leading engineering firms, NGOs, and government agencies in Kenya and beyond.

Vibrant Community

A supportive network of students, alumni, and faculty dedicated to engineering excellence.

What We Do

EESA's Mission

Bridging the gap between academic engineering and professional practice through structured programmes.

01

Workshops & Training

Hands-on technical workshops, coding bootcamps, and site visits organized throughout the academic year.

02

Engineering Projects

Collaborative multidisciplinary projects solving problems in energy, water, infrastructure, and agriculture.

03

Competitions

National and regional engineering competitions, exhibitions, and hackathons to showcase talent.

04

Industry Connection

Career fairs, mentorship programmes, and industrial attachments connecting students with employers.

05

Community Service

Using engineering skills for social good — clean water projects, rural electrification, and technology access.

06

Resource Library

Curated digital library of past papers, lab manuals, tutorials, and project references for all departments.
